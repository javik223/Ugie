History

Over 600 Years of Peace, Prosperity &
Cultural Uniqueness

The Ugieoba is a festival heralding good luck. It began in the era of Oba Ewuare the Great who reigned over the Benin Empire in the 15th century (1440 - 1473 AD). It is celebrated by the reigning Oba and Bini sons and daughters to mark the end of the Benin year, with prayers for peace and prosperity being offered for the coming year; it is a time of thanksgiving. This colourful festival which features the best of Benin life and culture is one of the rare occasions when the Oba dances in public.

The Ugie is a 14 day event of elaborate traditional and cultural activities, with participation from the different people that make up the Benin kingdom. It is a unique time and the only time in the year when the chiefs dress in their red regalia to dance the Ugie dance and pay homage to the Oba. It is also a period that features a rare appearance of the obaâ€™s wifes with their unique okuku hairdo.

The various ceremonies celebrated during the Ugie in Benin are commemorative of various events in the history of the Edo people, some of which are: Ugie ERHA OBA, Ugie EMOBO, Ugie EDOHIA, Ugie EWERE.

The Ugie has however endured and continues to retain its main features despite modernisation in all aspects of political, economic, sociological and technological development. The Benin Kingdom still pays so much attention to the festival which promotes the cultural values and unites the sons and daughters of Benin worldwide.
